Breaking and aligning displays

This is a brief practice for breaking and aligning displays in \(\TeX\).

The practice consists of a sequence of breaking challenges. You'll be asked to break at a given location, marked in red.

Your job is two-fold:

  1. First, identify how the alignment should look. This step is entirely mental; identify how the alignment should look.
  2. Then, make the changes to the existing TeX code to try and get the TeX to break how you identified it should.

The rules

While breaking displays can be challenging, the rules are few and relatively straightforward.

The authoritive reference for rules is the AMS Journal Style Guide. For our purposes, we will be concerned with Section 13.17, Breaking and aligning equations in displays.

Problem 5

\begin{align*} \oint_{C} (L \, \mathrm{d}x + M \, \mathrm{d}y) = \iint_{D} \left(\frac{\partial M}{\partial x} \mathbin{\color{red}+} \frac{\partial L}{\partial y}\right) \, \mathrm{d} A \end{align*}

Do note that, in this problem, we would be better off breaking at the "\(=\)". However, we are specifically asking you to instead break at the "\(+\)".
